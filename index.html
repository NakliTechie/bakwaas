About Bakwaas

बकवास Hindi/Urdu, noun — nonsense, drivel, hot air, chitchat. The stuff you say a lot of but rarely re-read.

Bakwaas is a browser-native explorer for your social-media archives. It started as a click-to-delete tool for Twitter/X, but it turns out the harder problem isn't deleting — it's finding what's worth deleting in 200,000 tweets. So mostly it's now an explorer that happens to make deletion easy at the end. Mastodon archives are supported too (engagement counts aren't in their exports, so engagement-dependent cards quietly degrade).

Everything runs locally. Nothing leaves your tab. No server, no accounts, no API keys to X, no telemetry. Even Wrapped exports are downloads from your browser, not uploads from it.

What's in each tab

  • StatsCounts, top hashtags / mentions / sources / languages, temporal heatmap (GitHub-contributions style across your full archive), hour × day-of-week, engagement distributions.
  • SearchFast full-text over tweets and likes via MiniSearch. Filter by originals / replies / retweets / media; flip scope between tweets, likes, both.
  • PatternsAlgorithmic deep dive: viral tweets, hashtag & source ROI, burst hashtags (era detection), activity change-points, longest streak & longest silence, verbosity drift, emoji mood timeline, top phrases (bigrams), top link hosts, sleep / quiet-hours by year, style fingerprint + archetype, self-reply threads, co-mention pairs (force-directed graph), first & last and top tweet per year, zero-engagement cleanup pile.
  • WrappedSpotify-Wrapped-style poster card for any year or all-time. Top tweet, top hashtag, peak month, era, posting personality. Download as standalone HTML (hostable anywhere) or PNG (postable to social).
  • TopicsHashtag co-occurrence clusters — themes you tag together. Drag the threshold slider to widen or tighten clusters.
  • SelectionThe confirmation tray. Everything you've opened to delete or marked deleted ends up here.

How deletion works

Bakwaas never calls X's API. To delete a tweet you click it open on X, delete it there in the real UI, then come back and confirm. Manual, on purpose. The hard part — knowing what to delete — happens here. The actual delete happens in X's own UI.

Get your archive

Twitter / X: x.com/settings/download_your_data → wait ~24 hours → drop the ZIP, or use "Open unzipped folder" (Chrome/Edge) for the FSA path with a sidecar state file written alongside the archive.

Mastodon: on your instance → Preferences → Import and Export → Request your archive → wait a few minutes → drop the ZIP. Mastodon exports don't include engagement counts on your own posts, so engagement-driven cards (Top viral, hashtag ROI) will be quiet — everything else works.
